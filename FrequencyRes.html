Key Concepts

  • Frequency Resolution Δf = 1/(N·Ts) — the spacing between DFT bins equals the reciprocal of the total record length. More samples → finer resolution.
  • Spectral Leakage — when a sinusoid does not fall exactly on a DFT bin, energy "leaks" into neighbouring bins, smearing the spectrum.
  • Hamming Window — multiplying the time samples by w[n] = 0.54 − 0.46·cos(2πn/(N−1)) tapers the ends to zero, greatly reducing leakage. The main lobe widens slightly but side-lobes drop by ~40 dB.
  • Try this: Set f₂ = 1.5 Hz (between bins) with Rectangular and watch the leakage. Switch to Hamming to see the improvement.
