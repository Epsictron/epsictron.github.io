My Take
Deep learning is powerful — and keeps getting more so.
But signal processing is a classic, and classics never go out of style.

Over the past decade, deep learning has surged ahead — outpacing traditional signal processing on benchmark after benchmark, from speech recognition to radar classification. The raw empirical power is undeniable, and I find it genuinely exciting.

Yet I believe the classical toolkit — Fourier analysis, convolution, stationarity, noise models, filter design — isn't being replaced. It's becoming the lens through which we understand why neural networks work. Convolutions encode translation invariance the same way FIR filters do. Attention is a learned, data-driven correlation. Residual connections echo feedback in recursive systems.

Signal processing gives you the intuition; deep learning gives you the scale. Together, they're sharper than either alone.
