Key Concepts

  • Zeros — a zero at z = rejθ forces H(z) = 0 at that point. When evaluated on the unit circle (z = ejω), the frequency ω = θ gets completely cancelled, creating a null.
  • Conjugate Pairs — real-coefficient filters must have zeros in conjugate pairs: z and z*. Both zeros together give H(z) = (z − rejθ)(z − re−jθ) = z² − 2r·cos(θ)·z + r².
  • Unit Circle & Perfect Nulls — a zero exactly on the unit circle (r = 1) creates a perfect null (−∞ dB) because the distance from the evaluation point ejθ to the zero is exactly zero. Moving the zero inside/outside (r < 1) makes the notch finite.
  • Try this: Drag θ across the range and watch the notch sweep through the spectrum. Then reduce r to 0.7 and see the null become a dip instead of a perfect zero.
