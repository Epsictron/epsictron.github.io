Key Concepts

  • Nyquist–Shannon Theorem — a band-limited signal with max frequency fmax can be perfectly reconstructed from samples if the sampling rate fs ≥ 2fmax. The threshold 2fmax is called the Nyquist rate.
  • Aliasing — when fs < 2fmax, high-frequency components fold back to lower frequencies in the sampled signal, producing a phantom frequency falias = |f − k·fs| for the nearest integer k.
  • Sinc Interpolation — ideal reconstruction convolves the samples with a sinc function: x(t) = Σ x[n]·sinc((t − nTs)/Ts). This demo uses sinc interpolation to show the reconstructed waveform.
  • Try this: Set f = 5 Hz and slowly lower fs from 40 Hz. Watch the reconstruction degrade as fs crosses the Nyquist rate (10 Hz), and observe the alias frequency.
